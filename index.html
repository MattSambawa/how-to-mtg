Introduction to Magic the Gathering

Explore lore rich worlds, discover unique strategies, and battle against your friends! Magic: The Gathering is a trading card game that can be played with two or more players.

With over 27,000 unique cards, there are infinite ways to play and various formats to choose from. One of the most popular formats is called Standard.

Check out the card gallery



Basic Rules

  • Each player begins the game with a life total of 20. The goal is to reduce your opponent's life total to 0.
  • Standard Magic decks contain sixty cards. Each deck consists of different types of cards such as creatures, sorceries, and lands.
  • In order to cast spells, you need a resource called mana. Mana is produced by lands from your deck.
  • After casting creature spells, you place them on your side of the board to fight with your opponent! This part is known as the combat phase.
  • When combat resolves, the board enters the final phases and the turn is passed to your opponent.


Turn Order & Phases

Lands

Lands are the primary mana source of your deck. You may play one land a turn on your main phase.



Creatures

Creatures are the bread and butter of your deck. In order to push damage on your opponent, you need to construct a board of minions.



Instances & Sorceries

Instances and sorceries are ways to interact with you and your opponent's board state. These interactions include things such as buff spells and damaging spells.

Instances are unique in the way that they can be used in between phases during your opponent's turn.
